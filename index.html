Problem Scenario

Coral reef ecosystems are rapidly declining due to rising ocean temperatures, pollution, and human activities. Scientists are exploring biotechnology-based coral restoration techniques to prevent irreversible damage.

Root Cause Analysis

The decline of coral reefs is primarily caused by climate change-induced ocean warming, chemical and plastic pollution, overfishing, and habitat destruction. These factors weaken coral resilience and accelerate bleaching events.

Analyze the Problem from Different Perspectives

Potential Consequences

  • Loss of marine biodiversity and ecosystem services
  • Economic impact on fisheries and coastal communities
  • Ethical risks of unintended ecological imbalance
  • Long-term sustainability challenges

How Can We Work on This Problem?

  • Encourage interdisciplinary research in marine biotechnology
  • Develop ethical guidelines for environmental interventions
  • Reduce pollution and greenhouse gas emissions
  • Strengthen conservation policies and public awareness
